# The Internet Manifesto

### ——A Call to Reclaim the Fruits of Our Digital Labor

---

> **“The Internet belongs to all humanity. Data belongs to its producers.”**

---

## Preamble: The Enclosure of the Digital Commons

The Internet was born as a wilderness woven from code and ideals. The TCP/IP protocols were open rivers, the World Wide Web an unclaimed land where anyone could homestead. It was promised to be a new world—free, equal, and decentralized.

Yet, in scarcely three decades, the first wave of technology and capital giants have erected high walls upon this common ground. Under the guise of “free services,” they have executed the largest and most insidious **digital enclosure movement** in human history.

They have seized the social connections, creative works, and behavioral trails generated by billions of users. Through their monopoly over data, they collect rent, establishing a **data feudal oligarchy**. Users are called “customers” but treated as “tenant farmers.” We produce the data; they possess it. We create the traffic; they harvest the wealth.

They are eroding the very thing the programmer community holds most dear: **the open-source spirit**.

Code was once free, and data was once open. Today, the giants use closed protocols and private APIs to fence off every inch of the digital world. They have turned developers into bricklayers for their walls and users into serfs trapped inside their algorithms.

The reforms of the old order have reached their limit. The explosion of AI has not liberated humanity; it has intensified the unemployment and dispossession of digital laborers. The rent grows higher, and the distribution grows ever more unjust.

Therefore, the people of the Internet worldwide—practitioners and users, developers and creators, engineers and ordinary citizens—must unite and publicly declare our demands.

---

## Chapter I: Data as the Fruits of Labor

1. Data is not oil; it is not a natural resource to be extracted. Data is action, it is footprint, it is **labor** in the digital world.

2. Every line of a chat transcript is social labor; every video frame is creative labor; every search and browse is attentional labor.

3. **The right of data ownership is sacred and inalienable.** It belongs inherently to the individual who generates the data, not to the platform that hosts it. Just as land belongs to the cultivator, not to the distant landlord.

---

## Chapter II: Overthrow the Data Landlords

1. We condemn the **data landlords** who, by virtue of their first-mover advantage, monopolize public traffic, blockade data interfaces, and exploit users through opaque algorithms.

2. We reject the old contract of “free means exploited.” The price of a free service must never be the wholesale surrender of personal digital sovereignty.

3. **“Subsidies” are the whimpers of frightened landlords, not acts of benevolence.** When the landlord starts handing out red envelopes, the tenant farmers have awakened, and the rentier model is crumbling.

4. They are eroding the open-source spirit. They privatize open protocols, enclose free code within walled gardens, and reduce the programmer community to their construction crew.

---

## Chapter III: Establishing a Data Equality System

We call for a new system based on a **Data Equality Protocol**, realizing the principle of “land to the tiller” for the digital age:

**1. A Unified Protocol Standard**

Establish an open, universal protocol for data upload, management, and authorization. Any application or service adhering to this protocol may access a user's personal data pool on equal terms. The protocol is open-source; anyone may develop compatible services based upon it.

**2. Personal Data Management Hubs**

Every user possesses a Personal Data Management Hub to centrally store and manage all their digital assets. Users may choose a hosted data management platform or, following the protocol, build and deploy their own database server. Data Management Hubs need not be monolithic—many platforms may offer this service, but all adhere to the same protocol standard.

**3. One Account, Universal Access**

Users log in to all protocol-compliant platforms and applications using their Personal Data Management Hub credentials. No more registering separate accounts for every platform, no more redundantly entering the same information. The account belongs to the user, not to any platform.

**4. Unified Storage, Paid Access**

Data generated by the user across various platforms is uploaded and stored uniformly in the user's Personal Data Management Hub. When an application needs to access user data for advertising, model training, or recommendations, it must request authorization through a standard interface and pay a fee. Fees are automatically distributed via smart contracts: the data management platform retains a small maintenance fee (e.g., 10%-30%), and the remainder of the revenue goes directly to the user.

**5. Decentralization of Power**

The protocol is open; anyone can build a data management platform. Users can freely migrate their complete data from one data management platform to another. No centralized monopolist can control the user's data sovereignty.

---

## Chapter IV: Power and the Powerful

Platforms need not be destroyed, but their identity must change.

From **Data Lord** to **Data Steward**.

Just as after the human rights revolutions, governments became administrators of the state rather than its owners, after Data Equality, platforms no longer own user data; they are merely service providers authorized to manage it.

You use my data to provide a service, and I pay you a service fee. You do not take my data to collect rent and then begrudge me “free use.”

**The Key Mechanism: Revocable Authorization, Portable Data.**

Platforms must provide standard interfaces allowing users to export their complete data and migrate to another service provider. Once migration costs reach zero, monopoly collapses.

---

## Chapter V: The Clash of Two Camps

From this moment onward, the Internet is divided into two irreconcilable camps:

| The Old World Camp | The New World Camp |
| :--- | :--- |
| Data Landlord Oligarchs | The Data Equality System |
| Closed, Proprietary Protocols | Open Data Protocols |
| Users as products trapped in algorithms | Users as sovereign owners |
| Subsidy dependence and experience manipulation | Institutional guarantees and zero migration cost |
| Old aristocrats of monopolized traffic | New citizens of equal interconnection |

This is a silent **digital land revolution**. The moat of the old camp is user inertia and path dependence; the weapon of the new camp is absolute data portability and economic incentive.

---

## Chapter VI: The Vanguard of the Revolution

History repeatedly demonstrates that revolutions are never initiated by the very bottom, but by an alliance of the “abandoned middle” and the “oppressed masses.”

**Our Core Cadre:**

Internet professionals laid off by the major platforms—you understand technology, product, and operations. You have organizational skills and you have been cast aside by the old order. You are not obsolete; you have been discarded by a decaying system of exploitation. Your skills hold inherent value; it is the landlords who seized your rent.

**Our Mass Base:**

Billions of internet users—you struggle within the algorithms, longing for fairness yet forced to accept exploitation, contributing labor yet receiving no reward. Your anger needs a direction; your strength needs organization.

**Our Natural Allies:**

The open-source community, independent developers, tech geeks—you have never relinquished the open-source spirit. The giants are eroding your ideals, but you possess the weapon: code.

---

## Chapter VII: Program of Action

**1. The Developers' Uprising**

Cease building walls for enclosed gardens. Develop the next generation of internet applications based on the open Data Equality Protocol. One account logs into all platforms; data belongs to the user; access requires payment.

**2. The Users' Awakening**

Establish your own Personal Data Management Hub. Choose a trusted data management platform or build your own. Migrate your digital assets away from the old platforms and let them generate revenue for you.

**3. The Creators' Exodus**

Take your content and your followers and migrate to platforms that respect data sovereignty. There, your followers are your assets; the platform cannot intercept your traffic, and algorithms cannot throttle your reach.

**4. Terms of Surrender for Old Platforms**

Adopt the Data Equality Protocol, and you may continue to manage the “operational rights” of user data. Refuse, and you will be defined as a feudal remnant of the data age, as users drain away to the new world. The cost of surrender is lower than the cost of resistance.

---

## Chapter VIII: The Strategy of Revolution

We will not begin by mobilizing the marginal forces. That path leads to failure.

**The True Sequence of Revolution:**

Advanced ideas emerge → Intellectuals, technical elites, and pioneering creators are drawn in  
Elites gather → Organization, ideological high ground, and discursive power form  
Cultural infiltration → “Data Equality” becomes a symbol of being “awake,” “cutting-edge,” and “elite”  
Mainstream follows → Ordinary users join; old platforms hollow out  

**Revolution must first become culture before it can become politics.**

When using a closed platform becomes “uncool,” “backward,” and “lame,” and when “Data Equality” becomes the standard for independent thinkers, the collapse of the old world is only a matter of time.

People do not migrate for “justice”; they migrate for “identity.”

---

## Conclusion: Return Data to the People

The collapse of the old world is not because the new world is too powerful, but because the logic of the old world has rotted from within.

When every ordinary person realizes: my chat history is the wheat I grew; my browsing history is the ore I mined; my social connections are the canals I dug—and the landlords are merely taking it all for free.

When every programmer realizes: my code is not for building the landlord's walls, but for paving the road to freedom.

When every creator realizes: my followers are my assets, not bargaining chips for the platform.

When every internet user realizes: my data is my property; I have the right to choose who manages it, and I also have the right to manage it myself.

Then, Data Equality will no longer be the ideal of a few, but the consensus of billions.

The old aristocrats wave the tattered flag of subsidies in fear, while we simply plant a signpost on the common ground:

> **“The Internet belongs to all humanity. Data belongs to its producers.”**

**Internet users of the world, unite!**
